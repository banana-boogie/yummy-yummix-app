# Thermomix Cooking Reference (TM5 / TM6 / TM7)

Comprehensive reference for Thermomix parameters, compiled from official Vorwerk/Cookidoo documentation and verified community sources.

## Speed Settings

| Setting | RPM | Best For |
|---------|-----|----------|
| **Spoon** | 40 | Gentlest stir; blunt side of blade, no cutting. Simmering, stews. |
| **Speed 1** | 100 | Very gentle stirring, slow cooking |
| **Speed 2** | 200 | Gentle stirring, slow cooking |
| **Speed 3** | 500 | Light mixing, slow cooking |
| **Speed 4** | 1,100 | Moderate mixing, start of chopping range |
| **Speed 5** | 2,000 | Chopping, moderate blending |
| **Speed 6** | 3,100 | Chopping, blending. **Max speed when heating above 60°C** |
| **Speed 7** | 4,400 | Fine chopping, blending |
| **Speed 8** | 5,800 | Fine blending, pureeing |
| **Speed 9** | 7,600 | Grinding, pulverizing |
| **Speed 10** | 10,200 | Maximum regular speed, smoothies, powdering |
| **Turbo** | 10,700 | Brief burst only, crushing ice |

### Speed Ranges

- **Spoon / 1-3** (40-500 RPM): "Cooking" speeds — gentle stirring, simmering, slow cooking, stews/casseroles without breaking ingredients.
- **4-6** (1,100-3,100 RPM): Chopping, moderate blending, making sauces. 4-5 for rough chops, 5-6 for finer.
- **7-10** (4,400-10,200 RPM): "Processing" speeds — pureeing soups, grinding nuts, smoothies, powdering sugar/spices.
- **Turbo**: Brief pulse only. Crushing ice, very quick hard grinding. **Never for hot food.**

### Spoon vs Speed 1

- **Spoon** (40 RPM): Blunt reverse side of blade. Zero cutting action. Best for stews, casseroles where ingredients must stay intact.
- **Speed 1** (100 RPM): 2.5x faster, uses sharp side (unless Reverse is on). Good for slow stirring sauces.

### Reverse Mode

Rotates blade counter-clockwise so the **blunt edge** leads instead of the sharp cutting edge.

**When to use:**
- Cooking stews/casseroles — stir without breaking vegetables/meat
- Cooking pasta — prevents blades from cutting it
- Sautéing onions/vegetables you want to stay in pieces
- Any cooking step where ingredients are already cut to size
- Slow cooking mode (mandatory: Reverse + max Speed 1 with blade cover)

**Always combine with low speeds** (Spoon, 1, or 2).

### Common Speed Mistakes

- Speed 5+ on delicate foods (mushrooms, cooked vegetables) — they'll be pulverized
- Not using Reverse when cooking stews/casseroles — sharp blade shreds ingredients
- Turbo or speed 7+ with hot food — dangerous splashing
- Over-chopping by setting too much time — start with 3-5 second bursts and check

## Temperature Settings

### Range

- **TM5/TM6 Manual mode**: 37°C to 120°C (adjustable in 1° increments)
- **TM7 Manual mode**: 37°C to 160°C (adjustable in 1° increments — extended range)
- **High Temperature mode** (TM6 only): Up to 160°C, Guided Cooking recipes only (not manual)
- **No temperature (null/cold)**: Chopping, grinding, blending, kneading — any task without heat

### Varoma

- **NOT a specific temperature number** — it's a steam-cooking mode
- With water/water-based liquid: generates maximum steam at ~100°C (boiling point)
- With oily ingredients: can reach ~120°C
- Purpose: produce steam for Varoma tray/dish above the bowl
- **250ml water evaporates in ~15 minutes** — use 500ml+ for 30-minute steaming
- **Max speed with Varoma: Speed 2** (current official guidance)

### Temperature Guide

| Temperature | Use Case |
|-------------|----------|
| **No temp (cold)** | Chopping, grinding, blending, kneading, whipping |
| **37°C** | Proofing yeast, warming baby food, tempering chocolate |
| **37-50°C** | Melting chocolate (50°C), melting butter, gentle warming |
| **50-60°C** | Dissolving sugar, warming sauces |
| **60-80°C** | Slow cooking, simmering sauces, custards, yogurt |
| **80-90°C** | Béchamel, thickening sauces, custards, poaching |
| **90-100°C** | Cooking rice, boiling water, pasta, jam |
| **100°C** | Full boil — soups, stews, casseroles, stocks |
| **100-120°C** | Sautéing, caramelizing onions, browning (manual mode max) |
| **Varoma** | Steaming vegetables/fish/meat, reducing sauces |
| **120-160°C** | High-heat browning/searing (TM6 Guided Cooking only) |

## Common Techniques with Settings

### Chopping/Mincing Vegetables

- **Rough chop**: Speed 4-5, 3-5 sec, no temp
- **Fine chop**: Speed 5-7, 5-8 sec, no temp
- **Hard vegetables** (carrots, celery): Speed 5-6, 3-6 sec
- **Soft vegetables** (tomatoes, zucchini): Speed 4-5, 2-4 sec
- **Herbs**: Speed 7-8, 3-5 sec
- **Tips**: Cut into equal-sized pieces first. Start with less time, check. Don't overfill.

### Chopping/Grinding Meat

- **Partially freeze first** (30-40 min in freezer). Cut into 1-inch cubes.
- Speed 6-7, 8-15 sec (check after 8 sec), no temp
- Mince continues breaking down during cooking — don't over-process.

### Kneading Dough

- Mix ingredients: Speed 6, 10-20 sec
- Knead: Dough mode, 2-5 minutes
- 2 minutes Thermomix ≈ 10 minutes by hand
- Max dough: 500g flour equivalent
- Speed must not exceed 4 for first 15 seconds
- Extended kneading heats dough — reserve 20% liquid to add cold during kneading

### Sautéing / Sofrito

1. Chop onions/garlic: Speed 5, 3-5 sec
2. Sauté: 100-120°C, Reverse, Speed 1-2 (or Spoon), 5-10 min
3. Add 10-50g oil
- Use simmering basket on lid (not measuring cup) above 95°C
- Caramelized onions: Varoma, Reverse, Spoon, 15-20 min

### Making Sauces / Soups

**Béchamel example:**
1. Melt butter: 2 min / 80°C / Speed 2
2. Cook roux: 2 min / 100°C / Speed 1
3. Add milk + seasonings: 6-10 min / 90°C / Speed 3-4

**Pureeing soup:** Speed 5 gradually to 7-8, 30-60 sec. Always start low with hot liquids.

### Steaming (Varoma)

- Min 500ml water in bowl (for up to 30 min)
- Varoma temperature, Speed 2
- Denser vegetables in Varoma dish (bottom), softer on tray (top)
- Cut denser vegetables smaller for even cooking
- Replace measuring cup with simmering basket or Varoma

### Emulsifying / Blending

- **Smoothies/purees**: Speed 7-10, 30-60 sec, no temp
- **Mayonnaise/hollandaise**: Speed 4-5, drizzle oil through lid opening
- **Hot soup**: Start speed 5, gradually increase to 7-8

### Melting Chocolate / Butter

- **Chocolate**: 50°C, Speed 1-2, 3-5 min (break into pieces first). **Never exceed 55°C.**
- **Butter**: 60-80°C, Speed 1, 2-3 min
- Tempering chocolate: Grate at Speed 9 for 7 sec, then melt at 50°C / Speed 2 / 4 min

### Cooking Rice / Grains

- **Rice Cooker mode** (TM6): Automatic
- **Manual**: 100°C, Speed 4 (or Reverse Speed 1), 18-22 min, measuring cup on
- Water ratio: ~1:1 to 1:1.2 (rice to water)
- ~70g raw rice per person

### Whipping Cream / Egg Whites

- **Requires butterfly whisk attachment**
- **Cream**: Speed 3-3.5, 1-1.5 min, no temp, no measuring cup
- **Egg whites**: Speed 3.5-4, 3-5 min, no temp, no measuring cup
- **Butterfly whisk max speed: 4**
- Bowl and whisk must be perfectly clean and grease-free

### Making Nut Butter

1. Grind: Speed 9, 30-60 sec
2. Process: Speed 5-7, 3-4 min (scrape down between stages)
3. Smooth: Speed 4-5, 2-3 min until creamy
4. Add 1 tbsp oil if needed. No temperature.

### Cooking Pasta

1. Boil water: 1200g water, 100°C, Speed 1, 9-12 min, measuring cup on
2. Add pasta through lid, add salt
3. Cook: 100°C, **Reverse**, Speed 1, per packet time, measuring cup **off** (steam escape)
4. Drain immediately — pasta keeps cooking in hot water

## Critical Safety Rules

### Temperature + Speed Restrictions

- **Above 60°C: Max speed 6.** TM6 auto-deactivates heating above speed 6.
- **Turbo: NEVER with hot food or liquid above 60°C**
- **Above 95°C: Replace measuring cup with simmering basket** (allow steam escape, prevent pressure)
- **Hot liquids: Always increase speed gradually** — never jump to high speeds
- **Varoma: Max Speed 2** (current official guidance)
- **Soups, casseroles, jams: Max 100°C** to prevent boiling over

### Lid / Measuring Cup Rules

- **Speed 7+**: Measuring cup MUST be in place
- **Temp 95°C+**: Replace measuring cup with simmering basket
- **Steaming (Varoma)**: Remove measuring cup, place Varoma on top
- **Whipping (butterfly whisk)**: Remove measuring cup for air incorporation

### Maximum Fill Levels

- **Bowl capacity**: 2.2 liters (marked inside bowl)
- **Hot foods expand** — leave extra headroom
- **Dough**: Max 500g flour
- **Browning/searing**: 100-250g per batch
- **Slow cooking meat**: Max 800g

## Cooking Modes

Named cooking modes that set specific parameter combinations automatically. The user selects the mode on their Thermomix screen.

| Mode | Code | TM5 | TM6 | TM7 | Parameters | Best For |
|------|------|-----|-----|-----|------------|----------|
| **Slow Cook** | `slow_cook` | No | Yes | Yes | Reverse, Speed 1, blade cover, 70-100°C, up to 12h | Stews, braises, casseroles |
| **Rice Cooker** | `rice_cooker` | No | Yes | Yes | Automatic temp/speed/time | Rice, grains |
| **Sous Vide** | `sous_vide` | No | Yes | Yes | Precise temp hold, no stirring | Proteins, vegetables |
| **Fermentation** | `fermentation` | No | Yes | Yes | Low temp hold (30-45°C), extended time | Yogurt, dough proofing, tempeh |
| **Open Cooking** | `open_cooking` | No | No | Yes | Lid-free, up to 100°C, temperature only — users stir manually with spatula | Risotto, sautéing, monitoring |
| **Browning** (Gentle / Intense) | `browning` | No | No | Yes (TM7 only) | 120-160°C, max 10min per step, NO speed setting. Gentle: vegetables, onions, delicate browning. Intense: searing meats, deep caramelization. Cannot be combined with Open Cooking — lid must be closed. | Browning, searing, caramelizing |
| ~~High Temperature~~ | `high_temperature` | No | Guided only | — | _TM6 Guided Cooking only. Superseded by `browning` on TM7. Do not use for recipe generation._ | — |
| **Dough** | `dough` | Yes | Yes | Yes | Kneading mode, max 500g flour | Bread, pasta, pizza dough |
| **Turbo** | `turbo` | Yes | Yes | Yes | Brief pulse at max speed | Crushing ice, quick grind |

### When to Use a Mode vs Manual

- Use a **named mode** when the Thermomix has a dedicated program for the technique — the user selects the mode from the screen.
- Use **manual mode** (no mode set) for standard cooking with time/speed/temp — the user sets parameters individually.
- On TM6, **high temperature** is only available via **Guided Cooking** (not for recipe generation). On TM7, use **browning** mode (gentle or intense) instead.
- **Browning cannot be combined with Open Cooking** — the lid must be closed for browning.

## TM7-Specific Features

The Thermomix TM7 (released 2024) adds several capabilities not available on TM5/TM6:

### Open Cooking Mode
- Cook **without the lid** at temperatures up to 100°C
- Lid does not lock until **Speed 2** — at Spoon or Speed 1, the lid sits freely
- Enables manual stirring, adding ingredients on the fly, and visual monitoring
- Great for risottos, sauteing, and any technique where you want to watch and intervene

### Extended Temperature Range
- Manual temperature up to **160°C** (vs 120°C on TM5/TM6)
- Enables proper browning, searing, and caramelization directly in the bowl
- No need for Guided Cooking mode to access high temperatures

### Browning Mode (TM7 Only)
- Two intensity levels: **Gentle** (vegetables, onions, garlic, delicate browning) and **Intense** (searing meats, deep caramelization)
- Temperatures up to 160°C, max 10 minutes per step
- Best results with small quantities (100-250g per batch)
- **Cannot be combined with Open Cooking** — lid must be closed for browning
- No speed setting — blade does not rotate in browning mode

### Fermentation Mode (also on TM6)
- Built-in fermentation function for yogurt, dough proofing, tempeh, etc.
- Maintains precise low temperatures over extended periods
- Available on both TM6 and TM7 (not TM7-exclusive)

### Other TM7 Improvements
- 10-inch touchscreen (larger and more responsive than TM6)
- Quieter motor
- Same 2.2L bowl capacity and blade system as TM6

## Cutter Disc / Cutter+

### TM5/TM6: Cutter Disc
- Accessory disc that sits on top of the blade inside the bowl
- Used for **uniform slicing and grating** of firm foods
- **Speed 4 only** — do not use at other speeds
- Maximum fill: **28 oz (approximately 800g)** — do not overfill
- Suitable foods: firm vegetables (carrots, zucchini, potatoes, beets), hard cheese, chocolate
- NOT suitable for: soft foods (tomatoes, bananas, bread), meat, leafy greens, very hard items (frozen food, nuts)

### TM7: Cutter+
- Redesigned accessory with improved cutting geometry
- Same usage rules: **Speed 4 only**, max 28 oz
- Enhanced grating results and easier cleaning
- Compatible only with TM7 bowl

### Maintenance
- Hand wash immediately after use — food residue hardens quickly
- Do not put in dishwasher (blade edges dull)
- Store separately from the bowl to protect cutting edges

### Steps That Should NOT Use Thermomix

- Deep frying / air frying
- Baking (makes dough but cannot bake)
- Roasting
- Making popcorn
- High-heat dry frying
- Pressure cooking
- Large-batch browning (>250g per batch — use a pan or skillet for larger quantities)
- Plating, garnishing
- Oven/grill tasks
- Manual shaping

### Additional Safety

- Never pour hot grease/oil into bowl alone (always with other ingredients)
- Never heat the bowl dry — requires moisture
- Never abruptly increase speed with hot food
- Scale accuracy: 0.1 oz increments, max 105 oz
